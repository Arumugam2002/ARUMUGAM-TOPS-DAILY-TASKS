Ashok LeyLand

Founded in 1948, Ashok Leyland is the second-largest commercial vehicle manufacturer in India, and the tenth-largest truck manufacturer globally. In 1955, the company changed its name from ‘Ashok Motors’ to ‘Ashok Leyland’ after collaborating with ‘Leyland Motors’ of England for the assembly of commercial vehicles.

Since then, the company has made considerable progress in revolutionising the commercial vehicles’ landscape of India. Today, the auto giant boast of at least one touchpoint within every 50 kilometres of the country. There are 9 state-of-the-art production units including, two international factories at Ras Al Khaimah in UAE, and Leeds in the United Kingdom.

Tata Trucks

Since its inception in 1954, Tata Motors has been at the forefront of technology and product innovations that have contributed significantly in facilitating the nation's growth. Today, it has emerged as the leader in Medium & Heavy Commercial Vehicles Truck category with 25 lakh trucks rolled out so far.

Mahindra Trucks

The Mahindra Group is a federation of companies that enables people to rise through innovative mobility solutions, driving rural prosperity, enhancing urban living, nurturing new businesses and fostering communities. It enjoys a leadership position in utility vehicles, information technology, financial services and vacation ownership in India and is the world’s largest tractor company, by volume.

It also enjoys a strong presence in agribusiness, aerospace, commercial vehicles, components, defense, logistics, real estate, renewable energy, speedboats and steel, amongst other businesses. Headquartered in India, Mahindra employs over 2 40 000 people across 100 countries.
